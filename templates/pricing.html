Why Zoho loses for study abroad

Zoho CRM is genuinely powerful — for a salesperson selling SaaS or manufacturing goods. But you're not selling products. You're guiding students through a 6–18 month journey with intake calendars, visa timelines, and document collection.

At 10 counsellors on Zoho Standard, you're already at ₹1.56L/year before WhatsApp, add-ons, or a Zoho partner.

Why Odoo loses for study abroad

Odoo is an ERP — designed for businesses managing inventory, manufacturing, and HR simultaneously. Running Odoo for a consultancy is like hiring a construction crane to move a piece of furniture.

Implementation alone can cost ₹50,000–₹3,00,000. And you still won't have intake alerts or a counsellor comparison report without custom development.

Why HubSpot loses for study abroad

HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely great — for basic contact management. But the moment you need reports or team management, you're on Starter at minimum and rapidly climbing to Professional (₹1,20,000+/year).

HubSpot has no concept of study abroad pipelines, intake windows, or counsellor accountability reports.

The Verdict

ApplyIQ is not the most powerful CRM in the world. But it is the most useful CRM for a study abroad consultancy in India. It does one job — study abroad lead management — and it does it better than any general-purpose platform at any price point.
